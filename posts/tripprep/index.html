Trip Preparation

For my trip to Europe, my preparation has been significantly more intensive than what would be considered “normal” on previous trips I’ve had. This is mainly because I have set a few goals for myself, some small, and some much larger.

For starters, I want to beat the jet lag - at least on my flight to Manchester. Our current itinerary has us leaving Atlanta at 6:00PM and arriving in Manchester just before 7:00AM. This means that I will need to sleep the entire flight to have energy for the day in Manchester. In order to achieve this, I have started to do a few things: one, I went to bed at around 9:00PM Monday night and woke up at around 3:00AM the next morning (the morning I am writing this :)

The next step is to repeat this same sleep routine tonight, and maybe even go to bed a little earlier. The goal here is to be tired an hour or two into the flight so I can sleep the rest of the way. I will then, assuming all goes as planned, continue to ease back into my normal sleep routine on GMT.

The next goal of mine is to take more video, and this means two separate things. I want to not only work on my video skills as a photographer, but in the spirit of documenting more, I want to get more video of everyday things happening, and me doing things as simple as spending time with my family. I am not sure about doing “vlog” style videos, as I am not particularly comfortable in front of the camera, however I will certainly be using my GoPro for significant amounts of footage.

Lastly, I want to continue to stay healthy on this trip, and get back into my workouts. This should be pretty easy to do, knock on wood…

So those are my goals, but how else am I prepping for this trip?

Once again, in the spirit of documenting things, I wanted to have access to mobile data while on this trip. I did some research, and Google’s Project Fi was the perfect option. The price breakdown is pretty straightforward. $20 for unlimited texting internationally (with varying rate of calls outside of North America), and $10 dollars for every gig of data up to 6 gigs. After the 6th gig, you get unlimited data, but it is throttled to a whopping 256k… If you want, you can enable speedy data again with the same price per gig coming back as well.

Of course, I also needed to pack all of my SD cards. These are all of them, plus a 128GB card currently in my camera, and another 32GB card in my GoPro.

Anyways, that is all for now :)
